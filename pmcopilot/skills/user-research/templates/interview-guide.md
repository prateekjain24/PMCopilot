## Warm-Up (5 minutes)

Build rapport and set the participant at ease. These questions are easy to answer and help establish conversational flow.

**Introduction script:**

"Thank you for taking the time to speak with us today. We are researching [topic] to help us build better tools for [audience]. There are no right or wrong answers -- we are interested in your honest experience. Everything you share will be kept confidential and used only for internal research purposes. Is it okay if we record this session for our notes?"

**Warm-up questions:**

1. [e.g., "Tell me a bit about your role. What does a typical week look like for you?"]
2. [e.g., "How long have you been in product management?"]

---

## Core Questions

### Topic Area 1: [e.g., "Current Research Workflow"]

*Suggested time: 10-12 minutes*

**Questions:**

1. [e.g., "Walk me through the last time you needed to research a competitor. What triggered it?"]
2. [e.g., "What tools or sources did you use? How did you decide where to look?"]
3. [e.g., "How did you organize or document what you found?"]

**Follow-up probes:**

- [e.g., "You mentioned [tool/source]. What made you choose that over alternatives?"]
- [e.g., "What was the hardest part of that process?"]
- [e.g., "How long did the whole process take?"]
- [e.g., "If you could have changed one thing about that experience, what would it be?"]

### Topic Area 2: [e.g., "Pain Points and Gaps"]

*Suggested time: 10-12 minutes*

**Questions:**

1. [e.g., "What frustrates you most about competitive research today?"]
2. [e.g., "Have you ever felt like you were missing important information? Tell me about that."]
3. [e.g., "How confident do you typically feel in the completeness of your research?"]

**Follow-up probes:**

- [e.g., "Can you give me a specific example of when incomplete research led to a problem?"]
- [e.g., "What would 'good enough' research look like for you?"]
- [e.g., "How does this compare to other parts of your job in terms of difficulty?"]

### Topic Area 3: [e.g., "Ideal Future State"]

*Suggested time: 8-10 minutes*

**Questions:**

1. [e.g., "If you had a magic wand, how would competitive research work for you?"]
2. [e.g., "What would make you feel confident that you had a complete picture of a competitor?"]
3. [e.g., "How would you want to share research findings with your team?"]

**Follow-up probes:**

- [e.g., "You mentioned [feature/capability]. How important is that relative to other things we discussed?"]
- [e.g., "Would you use that weekly, monthly, or only for specific projects?"]
- [e.g., "Who else on your team would benefit from this?"]

## Wrap-Up (5 minutes)

### Closing Questions

1. "Is there anything we did not cover that you think is important for us to understand?"
2. "Of everything we discussed today, what is the single biggest problem you would want solved?"
3. "Would you be open to a follow-up conversation if we have additional questions?"

### Participant Additions

"Before we wrap up, is there anything you would like to add or any questions you have for us?"

### Next Steps

- Thank the participant for their time
- Confirm compensation delivery method and timeline
- Let them know how their input will be used (in aggregate, anonymized)
- Provide contact information in case they think of something later

## Practical Guidance for the Interviewer

### Time Management

- Warm-up: 5 minutes
- Core questions: 30-35 minutes (split across topic areas)
- Wrap-up: 5 minutes
- Leave 2-3 minutes of buffer for transitions and overruns

### Active Listening Tips

- Let silences sit for 3-5 seconds before moving on; participants often fill silence with deeper insights
- Use mirroring: repeat the last few words of their statement as a question to encourage elaboration
- Avoid agreeing or disagreeing with statements; maintain neutral acknowledgment ("That makes sense," "Tell me more about that")
- Watch for emotional responses (frustration, excitement) and probe those moments

### Note-Taking

- Capture verbatim quotes whenever possible -- these are the most valuable artifacts
- Mark observations with tags: [pain point], [workaround], [feature request], [surprising], [emotional]
- Note non-verbal cues if on video: hesitation, enthusiasm, confusion
- Write down follow-up questions that come to mind without interrupting the participant

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Do not ask leading questions (e.g., "Do you find it frustrating when..." -- instead ask "How do you feel about...")
- Do not pitch or explain your product; this is a listening session
- Do not ask yes/no questions; reframe as open-ended
- Do not rush through questions to "get through the guide" -- depth matters more than coverage
